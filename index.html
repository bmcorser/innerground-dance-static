About Inner Ground Dance Company

Inner Ground Dance Company is Cornwall's contemporary dance company for people who are over 50.

The Company is led by two Artistic Directors, Jayne Devlin and Caroline Schanche. Jayne and Caroline take classes and workshops for members, choreograph our performance pieces, and lead the development of the Company.

The company is a constituted community organisation.

What Inner Ground Dance Company have done ...

Setting up the Company

In 2012, Jayne Devlin and Caroline Schanche identified a demand for high quality dance classes, workshops and performances for mature dancers in Cornwall. They held in that year two callout workshops for Inner Ground Dance Company, which were advertised through the local press in Cornwall, and the Internet.

These workshops introduced potential company members to the Artistic Directors, provided time to experience their working style and time to consider whether to join the company. The only criteria for joining were to be: aged over 50. and to commit to attending all the rehearsals. (It was understood that occasionally something might occur that would prevent attendance but the principal was established that members could not drop in and out of sessions as it suited them and that this commitment would underpin the high quality of the work.)

Of the 20 people who attended those workshops, nine people (all women) joined Inner Ground Dance Company, and Arts for Health Cornwall’s project ‘Moving Forward Looking Back’ provided the platform to launch the company. The company currently has around 15 members, both women and men.

Three New Dance Pieces

  • 2013 – I am Myself Again
  • 2015 – Line Trace Time
  • 2015 – The Table

In 2013, “Moving Forward Looking Back” (MoFoLoBa) won the Arts and Heath SW award in the Health category for its innovative approaches to health and well-being provision (Caroline was the project lead for the overall project). Inner Ground had been formed partly to provide a legacy for the project (along with the Visual Arts and Creative Writing elements of MoFoLoBa).

Through a year-long development with regular workshops, Inner Ground created their first performance piece – I am Myself Again – choreographed by Jayne Devlin. This was performed in 2013 as part of MoFoLoBa, at The Exchange Penzance; at Bristol City Hall for the AHSW award ceremony, and at Dartington Hall, for “Dance in Time” (UN Day of the Older Person).

In 2015 the second piece (choreographed by Caroline) Line Trace Time, was performed at Sterts Arts and Community Centre (Liskeard) as part of the Midsummer Dance Festival, at Tremenheere Sculpture Garden in Penzance, and at Dartington Hall (Dance in Time, 2015). Line Trace Time, and The Table (the third piece, choreographed by Jayne Devlin) were performed at AMATA (Falmouth University) as part of Mergecology in December 2015.

Over the 3 years since Inner Ground's creation, the company has hosted workshops run by Yael Flexer, Panta Rei, Helen Parlor Dance, NDT2, Emily Dobson and Rambert Dance Co. Each of these workshops had around 20 participants.

Inner Ground Dance Company artistic directors ...

Jayne Devlin

Jayne graduated from De Montfort University in 1994 and has 18 years experience working as a community dance artist as well as facilitating and participating in choreographic and performance projects. In addition to her work with Inner Ground Dance Company Jayne has received choreographic commissions from The Works Dance and Theatre Cornwall, Cscape Dance Company and Dance South West. These include a site specific piece for inclusion in, “The Venus Flower” 2011 and 2013, “Ocean Stars” and Pulse FX, and for the Olympiad Launch in Bodmin in 2009.

Her most recent commission was for the Arts for Health Cornwall ‘Home Service’ project, about which Phil Gibby, Area Director, South West, Arts Council England, said: 'This project is a great example of the impact of arts and culture on the wellbeing of individuals and its capacity for nurturing a sense of community.” This project was also mentioned in an article in the Guardian.

Jayne provides vibrant inclusive dance sessions for the regional Memory Cafes and specialist residential and day care providers for older people. She is dedicated to raising the profile and achievements of older dancers and to championing high profile opportunities for showcasing their work.

Caroline Schanche

Caroline trained in theatre at Dartington College of Arts (81-85). This course was grounded in the philosophy that the arts should be embedded in community life and available to all. She specialised in dance improvisation and Anatomical Release Technique under Mary Faulkerson’s internationally renowned leadership of the dance department.

After graduating she began working in London with marginalised groups such as adults with learning disabilities and elderly visually impaired people. Inspired by the positive impact of dance and movement with these groups she trained as a dance movement therapist at Hertfordshire College of Art and Design.

For the last 24 years she has lived and worked in West Cornwall running sessions for groups of all ages and abilities including pre-school children, medical students, stroke patients, people with learning disabilities and dementia.
